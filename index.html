Stakeholders, chokepoints, leverage

A simplified systems view highlights where India has the most meaningful levers.

Chokepoints and leverage

  • Hormuz closure: primary chokepoint for crude, LPG and fertilizers; leverage via rerouting, reserves, and diplomacy with Iran/GCC.[web:1][web:11][web:13]
  • Shipping & insurance: freight and risk premia; leverage via guarantees and diversified carriers.[web:3][web:11]
  • FX & inflation: imported inflation and CA gap; leverage via RBI reserves and calibrated fuel pricing.[web:6][web:21][web:24]

Insight: India’s most effective near‑term levers lie in fuel pricing, reserve use and targeted diplomacy, not in monetary tightening alone.

Policy priorities (15–60 days)

  1. Stabilise LPG and critical fuel supply, curb hoarding, and prioritise vulnerable users.[web:12]
  2. Lock in alternative crude and LNG routes while buffers last.[web:10][web:13]
  3. Communicate a clear RBI–MoF stance on FX and inflation to anchor expectations.[web:6][web:24]

Insight: A coherent short‑term package reduces market over‑reaction and buys time for structural diversification.
